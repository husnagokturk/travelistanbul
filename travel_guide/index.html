Discover Key Landmarks

Hagia Sophia

Originally constructed as a cathedral by Emperor Justinian I in 537, Hagia Sophia has served as a church, mosque, and museum before its recent reversion to a mosque. Its massive dome and stunning interior mosaics make it a masterpiece of Byzantine architecture.

Topkapi Palace

The opulent residence of Ottoman sultans from the 15th to the 19th centuries, Topkapi Palace is renowned for its elaborate courtyards, the Harem section, and the extensive collection of Ottoman treasures, including the Topkapi Dagger and the Spoonmaker’s Diamond.

Blue Mosque (Sultan Ahmed Mosque)

Built between 1609 and 1616, the Blue Mosque is celebrated for its striking blue Iznik tiles, grand dome, and six minarets. It remains an active mosque and a prominent symbol of Istanbul.

Yerebatan Cistern

Known for its massive marble columns and atmospheric lighting, this 6th-century Byzantine cistern once provided water to the city and is famous for its Medusa head columns and serene, subterranean environment.

Grand Bazaar (Kapalıçarşı)

This historical covered market, dating back to the 15th century, is a vibrant hub for shopping, with over 4,000 shops selling traditional crafts, textiles, and jewelry. Its labyrinthine layout adds to the charm and excitement of exploration.

Dolmabahçe Palace

Built in the mid-19th century, this palace showcases a blend of European and Ottoman architectural styles. Its opulent interiors and gardens reflect the wealth and grandeur of the late Ottoman Empire.

Galata Tower

Erected in the 14th century by the Genoese, the Galata Tower provides panoramic views of Istanbul’s skyline and the Bosphorus. It has been a key landmark for navigation and offers stunning vistas of the city.

Discover Must Have & Must Taste

Must Have

Turkish Delight (Lokum)

A traditional sweet treat available in various flavors and textures, often enjoyed with a cup of Turkish coffee.

Where to Buy: Hafız Mustafa 1864, Sirkeci, Istanbul
Handmade Ceramics

Beautifully decorated pottery and tiles, often featuring traditional Ottoman patterns. Great for souvenirs or home décor.

Where to Buy: Grand Bazaar, Sultanahmet, Istanbul
Evil Eye Amulets

Blue glass amulets designed to ward off negative energy and bring good luck. A popular and meaningful souvenir.

Where to Buy: Various shops in the Grand Bazaar and Spice Bazaar
Spices and Herbs

A variety of aromatic spices and herbs used in Turkish cuisine, such as saffron, sumac, and Turkish tea blends.

Where to Buy: Spice Bazaar, Eminönü, Istanbul

Must Taste

Baklava

A sweet pastry made of layers of filo dough filled with nuts and honey or syrup. A deliciously rich and indulgent treat.

Where to Try: Hafız Mustafa 1864
Simit

A circular bread covered in sesame seeds, often enjoyed with tea or cheese. A popular street food and breakfast item.

Dolma

Grape leaves stuffed with a mixture of rice, pine nuts, and spices. A flavorful and traditional Turkish dish.

Where to Try: Ciya Sofrası, Kadıköy, Istanbul
Mantı

Turkish dumplings filled with spiced meat and served with yogurt and garlic sauce. A comforting and savory dish.

Where to Try: Aşkana Mantı, Tuğbiş Mantı Evi

Location: Eminönü, located in the Spice Bazaar
Olden 1545

Specialties: Offers a unique dining experience with a focus on high-quality, flavorful dishes, including vegan options and gourmet meals.

Service Options: Dine-in
